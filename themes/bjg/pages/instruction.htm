Instruction

Our Philosophy

Foster a Relationship

We want to have a relationship with our students. What happens off the course very often impacts what happens on the course. We want to understand who our students are as people and athletes, how they learn best, and what their goals for golf and life are.

Develop a Plan

When we know our students and what their goals are, we can develop a tailored plan for improvement and sustained success. We don't give lessons, we provide coaching. We want to identify areas of improvement and continually work together toward those goals.

Reinforce Instruction

Anyone can stand on a range and hit good shots, but can you hit those same shots when you're on the course? We believe in taking our instruction off the range and onto the course as much as possible to reinforce what we're teaching students.

What Makes Our Instruction Different?

Hands-On Approach

We believe the best way to learn is by being moved into the correct positions. This helps our students develop a lasting, repeatable swing.

Short Game Focus

50% of the game happens inside 150 yards, so we stress the importance of pitching, chipping, and putting as a way to improve scores.

On-Course Learning

It's critical to learn under the conditions in which you'll play. To that end, we strive to teach students on the course as much as possible.
